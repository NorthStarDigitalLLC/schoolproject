CASE NO. 1818-VFM
The Trial of Victor Frankenstein
COURT OF MORAL JUSTICE · 1818

The Trial of
Victor Frankenstein

Victor Frankenstein is guilty because he created life and then abandoned it. His neglect directly caused the Creature's suffering and the deaths that followed.

Charges

Victor Frankenstein stands accused on three counts:

  1. Negligence as a creator.
  2. Willful abandonment.
  3. Indirect responsibility for the deaths of William, Clerval, and Elizabeth.
CONTEXT

Background of the Case

Victor Frankenstein is a scientist who discovers how to create life. After bringing the Creature to life, he is horrified by it and immediately abandons it. The Creature, left alone with no guidance, must learn about the world on its own and is repeatedly rejected by society.

Victor abandons the Creature the moment it comes to life. Instead of guiding or helping it, he runs away. This shows he failed his responsibility as a creator immediately.

EXHIBIT

FINAL
CONSEQUENCES

The results of Victor's actions.

You are my creator, but I am your master; obey!
The Creature

The Creature turns to violence after abandonment and rejection. The deaths of William, Clerval, and Elizabeth follow this pattern. This shows a clear chain of cause and effect, where Victor's actions directly lead to these outcomes.

The Defense vs. The Prosecution

The Defense Argues

The Creature is responsible because he makes conscious choices. He understands right and wrong, yet still chooses violence. After being rejected, he chooses revenge instead of restraint — he deliberately kills William, Clerval, and Elizabeth. These actions are intentional, meaning the responsibility lies with him.

vs
The Prosecution Responds

The defense focuses on the Creature's choices, but ignores the cause of those choices. The Creature was not born violent — he states, "I was benevolent and good; misery made me a fiend." Victor created life and then abandoned it without guidance, forcing the Creature into isolation and suffering. That suffering directly leads to his actions. This is a clear chain of cause and effect: Victor's neglect created the conditions that led to violence. Therefore, Victor is responsible.

CLOSING STATEMENT · FOR THE PROSECUTION

Victor Frankenstein is guilty because he created life but refused responsibility for it.

The Creature was not born evil, but shaped by neglect and rejection.

Victor had the power to guide his creation but failed to do so.

This chain of cause and effect shows that Victor's actions directly caused the outcome.

Victor had power, but failed to take responsibility for it.

THE COURT OF MORAL JUSTICE FINDS
The defendant, Victor Frankenstein —
GUILTY

Victor Frankenstein is guilty on all counts because his actions directly caused the outcome.
